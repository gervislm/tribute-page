Dr. Humberto Fernández-Morán

First man to introduce the concept of cryoultramicrotomy

Here's a time line of Dr. Fernández's life:

  • 1924 - Born in La Cañada de Urdaneta, Zulia, Venezuela.
  • 1944 - He graduated as a medical surgeon and doctor of medical sciences.
  • 1945 - Complete an internship in neurology and neuropathology at George Washington University.
  • 1946 - Did a neurosurgery residency at Serafimer Hospital in Stockholm with neurosurgeon Herbert Olivecrona.
  • 1947 - He worked as a researcher in the area of Electron Microscopy at the Nobel Institute of Physics.
  • 1948 - He worked as a researcher at the Institute for Cellular and Genetic Research, part of the Karolinska Institutet in Stockholm.
  • 1951 - He received a master's degree in cell biology and the following year his doctorate in biophysics from Stockholm University.
  • 1952 - He received his doctorate in biophysics from Stockholm University.
  • 1955 - He patented the diamond-tipped scalpel.
  • 1959 - He contributed to the use of cryofixation and low-temperature preparation techniques using helium II, applying them to the study of tissue ultrastructure. Likewise, he made important contributions to the knowledge of the structure of the nerve.
  • 1960 - He proposed for the first time to directly observe hydrated frozen samples (frozen-hydrated), building the first cryo-electron microscope and the first cryo-sample holder, with which he introduces the concept of cryo-electron microscopy being considered by Harvard University in the list of the 100 scholars who contributed the most to scientific development in the last century.
  • 1970 - He is hired by NASA to work on the Apollo project in the field of physical-chemical analysis of lunar rocks.
  • 1979 - Presented at the first Venezuelan and Latin American Congress of Neurosciences (Maracaibo 1979) the advances in research on the molecular organization of cell membranes analyzed with the proposed technique.
  • 1998 - The IVIC creates the Humberto Fernández Center for Structural Biology with the aim of interpreting biological phenomena at a molecular level through a wide range of studies of the structure of proteins, nucleic acids, membranes, organelles and viruses using techniques such as cryo-electron microscopy , digital image processing, nuclear magnetic resonance, small angle X-ray diffraction and X-ray crystallography.
  • 1999 - Fernández died in Stockholm, the product of a brain aneurysm.

Awards given:

  • Knighthood of the Order of the Polar Star, conferred by the King of Sweden.
  • Claude Bernard Medal, awarded by the University of Montreal.
  • Physician of the Year Award, granted by the University of Cambridge.
  • John Scott Medal 1967.
  • Special recognition given by NASA on the occasion of the X anniversary of the Apollo program.
  • Humberto Fernández Structural Biology Center of the IVIC.
  • State Basic School "Humberto Fernández" in Maracaibo.
  • (196476) Humfernandez: main-belt asteroid named in honor of Humberto Fernández, discovered on May 2, 2003 by astronomers Ignacio Ferrín and Carlos Leal at the National Astronomical Observatory of Llano del Hato, located in the Cordillera de Mérida, Venezuela.
